Ethnicities & Regions

Malgra (Water)

Riverside dwellers with stilt houses and fish ponds. Their festivals follow the moon and tides. Known for Aeolian and Hirajoshi scales, water bells, and tuned stones.

Gezhgia (Earth)

Live in wide savannahs, excel in ceramics and stained glass. Houses of earth with grand pointed roofs. Music leans on Dorian and Melodic Minor, using glass bonangs and keyed strings.

Lewauki (Wood)

Forest canopy people who evolved tree dwellings into soaring wooden homes. Their rituals revolve around seasonal shifts. Instruments are mostly bamboo and slit drums.

Chejye (Metal)

Mountain miners, living in white limestone multi-family houses. Prefer Ionian and Harmonic Major scales on flutes and bronze gongs.

Hlukhuyig (Air)

Plateau archers with wind kites and rotating turbines. Architecture airy and adorned with feather symbols. Use Lydian scales with mouth organs and open brass.

Serkha (Fire)

Live by volcanic slopes, hold rituals with sulfur and eternal flames. Music explores Phrygian and Ryukyuan modes on three-tone gongs and shield drums.
