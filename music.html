Musical Culture

Each ethnicity in Daur Wayah fosters distinct musical traditions:

  • Malgra: Aeolian, Hirajoshi, Hungarian Minor on boat lutes, water bells, and tuned stones.
  • Gezhgia: Dorian, Melodic Minor, In Sen with glass bonangs, gendher, and nyckelharpa-like instruments.
  • Chejye: Ionian and Harmonic Major, employing flutes, bronze gongs, and jaw harps.
  • Levra: Mixolydian and Lydian Dominant on slit drums and tilted zithers.
  • Hlukhuyig: Lydian and Acoustic scales with mouth organs, open brass, and bamboo resonators.
  • Serkha: Phrygian, Ryukyuan, and Neapolitan Minor on three-tone gongs and shield drums.

During the Segmentation Age, rigid scales broke down. Hybrid genres, 17-tone equal divisions, and cross-ethnic festivals flourished, turning Bird Island into a canvas of ever-evolving sound.
