Calendar Systems

Daur Wayah’s societies employ multiple calendar systems:

  • MK Calendar (Mandate of Kelana): 13 months × 28 days (364 days), with 1-2 “Leap Days” outside the week structure to align with the solar year.
  • Southern Lunar Calendar: used by Malgra, Levra, and Gezhgia, following 12 lunar months (~354 days), sometimes adding a day in the last month.
  • Northern Lunisolar Calendar: used by Chejye, Serkha, and Hlukhuyig, inserting a 13th month in a 19-year cycle to keep festivals aligned with seasons.
  • Pranata Mangsa-like Solar Seasons: especially among Lewauki and Gezhgia farmers, dividing the year into variable-length agricultural seasons.
